Private Pay + Out-of-Network

Self-pay therapy can offer more flexibility

Some clients choose to self-pay for increased flexibility, privacy, and a more tailored therapeutic experience without insurance restrictions.

A superbill can be provided upon request for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Reimbursement depends on your specific plan and benefits.

Why some clients choose private pay

  • No diagnosis requirement for insurance purposes
  • Greater privacy and confidentiality
  • Flexibility in session frequency and treatment approach
  • Continuity of care without insurance limitations or approvals

Good Faith Estimate

Your right to cost transparency

Under the No Surprises Act, you have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate explaining the expected cost of services. This helps ensure full transparency around your financial investment in therapy.

You are also welcome to reach out directly with questions about rates, insurance, or the process of getting started.

Helpful next steps

  • Contact your insurance provider to confirm mental health benefits.
  • Ask whether you have a deductible or copay.
  • Ask whether out-of-network reimbursement is available.
  • Ask whether preauthorization or referrals are required.

Rates + Insurance FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How much does an individual therapy session cost?

An individual therapy session is 50 minutes and costs $150.

What insurance providers are accepted?

Get Real Therapy is in-network with Aetna, Anthem, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna and Evernorth, Florida Blue, Humana, and TRICARE. UnitedHealthcare and Optum are listed as coming soon.

Can I self-pay for therapy?

Yes. Some clients choose private pay for increased flexibility, privacy, and a more tailored therapeutic experience without insurance restrictions.

Can I receive a superbill?

A superbill can be provided upon request for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

Have questions about rates or insurance?
