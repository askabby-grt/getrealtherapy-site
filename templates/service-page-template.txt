SECTION CONTENT REQUIREMENTS

1. Problem Recognition Section
Purpose: Help the visitor feel understood.
Must include:
- H2 naming the lived experience
- 3 to 5 paragraphs
- side card with symptom list
Tone:
- validating
- non-shaming
- emotionally specific
- no diagnosis claims

2. Context / Why This Happens Section
Purpose: Explain without overpromising.
Must include:
- nervous system / emotional pattern / attachment / stress context where appropriate
- 3 cards with common life contexts
- one internal link to Who This Is For or a related service

3. What Therapy Helps With Section
Purpose: Explain outcomes as possibilities, not guarantees.
Must include:
- H2 focused on support
- side card with "In therapy, we work toward:"
- 6 to 9 bullets
Avoid:
- "cure"
- "guaranteed"
- "fix permanently"

4. Approach Section
Purpose: Show credibility and depth.
Use 3 cards:
- Internal Family Systems (IFS)
- EMDR Therapy
- AEDP Therapy
Or adapt for service if needed.

Each card should be 2 to 3 short paragraphs.

5. What to Expect Section
Purpose: Reduce anxiety about starting.
Must include:
- supportive copy
- links to /what-to-expect and /who-this-is-for

6. Reassurance Section
Purpose: Encourage early support before crisis.
Must include:
- "You do not need to wait..."
- ethical, calm, grounded language

7. Related Resources Section
Purpose: SEO and internal funnel.
Use resource-grid with 4 to 6 cards.
Each card must include:
- title
- short description
- tracked link

Use related_blog_click for blog links.

8. FAQ Section
Use .faq-list and .faq-item.
Must include 4 to 6 FAQs.
FAQ copy must match FAQ schema exactly.
FAQ items should be visible by default unless an accordion pattern already exists globally.

9. Final CTA Section
Use service-section--plum and final-cta.
Include:
- emotional H2
- short paragraph
- Book Consultation CTA
- Start Your Healing Journey CTA

MOBILE / DESKTOP UX STANDARD
Ensure:
- hero grid works on desktop
- hero stacks on mobile
- buttons are full-width on mobile through global CSS
- cards stack on mobile
- no horizontal overflow
- content is not too dense
- paragraphs are not too long
- side cards are scannable

ACCESSIBILITY STANDARD
Ensure:
- one H1 only
- headings follow order
- links are descriptive
- images have alt text
- skip link exists
- button text is meaningful
- no empty anchors
- no unlabeled form fields if form exists

ETHICAL THERAPY COPY STANDARD
Do:
- validate experience
- use warm grounded language
- say "may help" and "can support"
- encourage consultation
- avoid shame
- avoid urgency manipulation

Do not:
- promise cures
- guarantee outcomes
- diagnose visitors
- imply therapy replaces emergency support
- use fear-based copy
- exaggerate conditions

INTERNAL LINKING REQUIREMENTS

- /book-consultation
- /contact
- /what-to-expect
- /who-this-is-for
- at least one related service
- at least 3 related blog/resource pages
- /blog or /resources
